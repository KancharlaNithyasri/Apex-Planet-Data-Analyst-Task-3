# Task 3 - Step1: Core KPI Definitions (Sample-Superstore Analysis)

## Project Overview
This document formally establishes the 4 core Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) used to track the financial and operational health of the Superstore.
These metrics ensure that business decisions align with regional profitability and sales optimization strategies.

1. Total Sales Revenue
         Description: The gross monetary value generated by all customer orders across all product categories and regions.
   Mathematical Formula:
    Total Sales = Sum of all line-item Sales values
Technical DAX Measure:
```dax
    Total_Sales = SUM('Sample - Superstore'[Sales])
    ```
*Business Rationale: This serves as the top-line benchmark for the entire business.
It allows regional managers to identify high-demand zones and track quarterly or yearly growth trends.



2. Overall Profit Margin
      Description:The percentage of gross sales revenue that the Superstore retains as actual profit after fulfilling all product costs.
Mathematical Formula:
    Profit Margin = (Total Profit / Total Sales) * 100
Technical DAX Measure:
```dax
    Profit_Margin = DIVIDE(SUM('Sample - Superstore'[Profit]), [Total_Sales], 0)
    ```
*Business Rationale:This metric is absolutely vital for the Sample-Superstore dataset.
Historically, categories like 'Furniture' (especially Tables) and regions like the 'Central' US display very high Sales numbers but negative Profits due to aggressive discounting.
Tracking the Profit Margin ensures the company is growing sustainably, not just losing money on high volumes.



3. Average Order Value (AOV)
      Description: The average amount of money a customer spends during a single checkout transaction.
Mathematical Formula:
    Average Order Value = Total Sales / Total Number of Unique Orders
Technical DAX Measure:
```dax
    Average_Order_Value = DIVIDE([Total_Sales], DISTINCTCOUNT('Sample - Superstore'[Order ID]), 0)
    ```
*Business Rationale: AOV helps the marketing team evaluate purchasing patterns. If AOV drops, it signals that customers are buying cheaper individual items.
The business can use this insight to introduce cross-selling strategies, such as bundling accessories with Technology items or running "spend $50 get free shipping" campaigns.



4. Product Return Rate
     Description:The proportion of total orders placed that are sent back by customers.
Mathematical Formula:
    Return Rate = (Count of Returned Orders / Total Count of Orders) * 100
Technical DAX Measure:
```dax
    Return_Rate = DIVIDE(COUNT('Sample - Superstore'[Returned]), COUNTA('Sample - Superstore'[Order ID]), 0)
    ```
*Business Rationale: High returns represent an operational and financial leakage. Tracking this metric points out systemic issues,
 such as specific products arriving damaged or descriptions misleading the buyers, which heavily penalizes net margins.
